<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【鼠年特辑】事业“鼠来宝” 学业“鼠一鼠二”</h2>
<img width="600" src="http://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2020/01/GettyImages-84134688-600x400.jpg" />
<h6>2020年是十二生肖里的鼠年。鼠也是十二生肖里排名第一的动物。图为2008年12月26日，东京阳光国际水族馆举行的活动。（TOSHIFUMI KITAMURA/AFP via Getty Images）
</h6>
<hr>
<p>【大纪元2020年01月06日讯】2020年是十二生肖里的鼠年。鼠也是十二生肖里排名第一的动物。</p>
<p>The year 2020 is the year of the mouse. It ranks number one out of the twelve animal signs.</p>
<p>● 为何“鼠”排名十二生肖第一？<br />
Why does the Mouse rank No.1 out of the 12 animals?</p>
<p>老鼠是所有十二生肖中的第一个。<br />
根据神话，玉皇大帝说，十二生肖排名的顺序，将由动物们的渡河比赛，抵达终点的顺序来决定。<br />
老鼠哄骗了牛，得以在牛背上搭便车渡河。<br />
然而，一抵达终点时，老鼠率先跳下牛背，在牛之前抵达终点，成为排名第一。<br />
The Rat is the first of all zodiac animals.<br />
According to one myth, the Jade Emperor said the order would be decided by the order in which they arrived to his party.<br />
The Rat tricked the Ox into giving him a ride.<br />
Then, just as they arrived at the finish line, Rat jumped down and landed ahead of Ox, becoming first.</p>
<figure id="attachment_11772406" style="width: 450px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="http://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2020/01/GettyImages-84134692.jpg"><img src="http://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2020/01/GettyImages-84134692-450x601.jpg" alt="A calf and a hamster are displayed during a ceremony held to mark the passage from the Chinese &quot;Year of Rat&quot; to the &quot;Year of Ox&quot; at the Sunshine International Aquarium in Tokyo on December 26, 2008. AFP PHOTO / TOSHIFUMI KITAMURA (Photo credit should read TOSHIFUMI KITAMURA/AFP via Getty Images)" width="450" b="601" class="size-medium wp-image-11772406" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">传说鼠哄骗了牛，得以在牛背上搭便车渡河。然而，一抵达终点时，老鼠率先跳下牛背，在牛之前抵达终点，成为排名第一。图为2008年12月26日，东京阳光国际水族馆举行的活动。（TOSHIFUMI KITAMURA/AFP via Getty Images）</figcaption></figure>
<p>● 鼠年吉祥话/shǔ nián jí xiáng huà<br />
Blessing words in the year of the mouse</p>
<p>(1) “鼠”来宝（数来宝）/shǔ lái bǎo<br />
原意：一种曲艺形式，现多用竹板打拍，边敲边唱，一般是即兴编词。<br />
Original Meaning：A type of artistic performing, referring to rhythmic story-telling to clapper accompaniment by using finger clappers.</p>
<p>吉祥意/jí xiá yì：老“鼠”招“来”“宝”物/lǎo &#8220;shǔ&#8221; zhāo &#8220;lái&#8221; &#8220;bǎo&#8221; wù<br />
Lucky Meaning：When the mouse comes, the valuables and fortune come as well.</p>
<p>(2) “鼠”一“鼠”二（数一数二）/shǔ yī shǔ èr<br />
原意：出类拔萃/chū lèi bá cuì<br />
Lucky Meaning：Extremely good or impressive performance</p>
<p>(3) 财源广进、“鼠”不胜“鼠”（数不胜数）/cái yuán guǎng jìn、shǔ bù shēng shǔ<br />
原意：钱财滚滚而来，数量多到数不完。<br />
Lucky Meaning：Money rolling in and having no time to count the number</p>
<p>(4) 神仙眷“鼠”（神仙眷属）/shén xiān juàn shǔ<br />
原意：比喻婚姻美满、感情和睦的夫妇。<br />
Lucky Meaning：Life between the married couple full of happiness and harmony like heavenly immortals</p>
<p>(5) 如“鼠”家珍（如数家珍）/rú shǔ jiā zhēn<br />
原意：比喻对事物的熟悉与清楚，好像计数自家所珍藏的宝物一般。<br />
Lucky Meaning：Able to understand things completely and thoroughly, like counting what we have owned </p>
<p>(6) 有情人终成眷“鼠”（有情人终成眷属）/yǒu qíng rén zhōng chéng juàn shǔ<br />
原意：有感情的恋人，终于得以结合，成为夫妻。<br />
Lucky Meaning：All shall be well, Jack shall have Jill. (Lovers get married eventually.)</p>
<p>(7) “鼠”（数）不尽的钞票、快乐、收获/shǔ bù jìn de chāo piào、kuài lè、shōu huò<br />
原意：用不完的钱、时时开心、收获满满<br />
Lucky Meaning：Having countless money, plenty of happiness, and plentiful harvest.</p>
<p>● 鼠，又称老鼠，“鼠”的种类：家鼠、宠物鼠、仓鼠、天竺鼠、土拨鼠、黄鼠狼等。<br />
shǔ, also called &#8220;lǎo shǔ&#8221;(&#8220;Old&#8221; Mouse literally).<br />
There are types of mice, such as：jiā shǔ (Black Rat), chǒng wù shǔ (Pet Mouse), cāng shǔ<br />
 (Hamster), tiān zhú shǔ (Guinea Pig), tǔ bō shǔ (Groundhog), huáng shǔ láng (Weasel).</p>
<p>事实上，上述的众多鼠种，只有黄鼠狼不是鼠类。<br />
In fact, the above mentioned, only huáng shǔ láng (Weasel) does not belong to the same kind.</p>
<p>● 俗谚说，“黄鼠狼给鸡拜年”，寓意：没安好心！huáng shǔ láng gěi jī bài nián：méi ān hǎo xīn<br />
An old Chinese saying, &#8220;when the fox preaches, look to the geese.&#8221;</p>
<p>● 生肖属“鼠”的性格<br />
Personality and Characteristics when born in the year of the mouse</p>
<p>乐观、充满活力！老鼠年出生的人――受众人爱戴。<br />
容易察觉他人的情绪，但也固执己见。<br />
友善，但因为缺乏沟通能力，常给人不礼貌和粗鲁的感觉。<br />
Optimistic and energetic, people born in the Rat year are likable by all.<br />
They are sensitive to other&#8217;s emotions but are stubborn with your opinion.<br />
Their personality is kind, but due to weak communication skills, their words may seem impolite and rude.</p>
<p>财务上，属鼠的人喜欢储蓄，可能有些小气。<br />
但是，喜欢囤积物品的倾向，有时会浪费钱在不需要的东西上。<br />
On the financial side, they like saving and can be stingy.<br />
However, their love for hoarding will sometimes cause them to waste money on unnecessary things.</p>
<p>(1) 肖鼠男士/Male Rat</p>
<p>属鼠的男士们很聪明，都能迅速适应新环境。<br />
具备创造力并善用机会。<br />
但是，有时又缺乏勇气。<br />
虽然有很好的主意，但是可能较不适合担任领导职务。<br />
Men born in the Rat year are clever and adapt quickly to new environments.<br />
They are creative great at taking advantage of opportunities.<br />
However, they sometimes lack the courage to do so. Although they have great ideas, they might not be suitable for leadership positions.</p>
<p>(2) 肖鼠女士/Female Rat</p>
<p>属鼠的女士们可谓传统女性。<br />
做事井然有序，并以家庭为重。<br />
妥善照顾家里的一切，丈夫无须额外操心。<br />
除了家庭以外，也具备责任感与处事能力。<br />
Women born in the Rat year are the traditional women.<br />
They love keeping things organized and place great value on the family.<br />
Everything is taken care of by them and there is no need for their husband to worry.<br />
Outside of home, they&#8217;re also someone with a sense of responsibility and ability.#</p>
